Copenhagen City Tourism Logo

A memorable logo for the city dubbed the happiest in the world.

Introduction

For this project, I was tasked with creating a logo for a city of my choosing. As you can see, I chose Copenhagen. Copenhagen is known for its eco-friendly initiatives and technology, its Tivoli Gardens, Nyhavn Harbor, and much more. It is also the capital city of Denmark. Denmark ranks highest in the world for happiness! This is believed to be linked to danish people's feelings of social equality, individual freedom, and community spirit. Danish design is also well-known to be functional, but warm and inviting.

Capturing “Hygge”

Hygge is a Danish word used when acknowledging a feeling or moment, whether alone or with friends, at home or out, ordinary or extraordinary as cosy, charming or special. This feeling is what I strived to represent in the final logo.

Real Life Inspiration

Historical Relevance

The Tivoli Gardens amusement park opened in 1843 with the purpose of lifting people's spirits. The garden features many novel attractions and details. One of these details were the engenius blackout lamps designed in 1941 by Poul Henningsen. The unique design of these lamps illuminated Tivoli Gardens while remaining undetectable by overhead aircrafts, allowing the gardens to remain open until midnight. It's a celebrated symbol of Danish design because of its history of providing the city joy while under German occupation.

A City of Engenuity & Joy

Execution

Copenhagen is a city progressive in technology, architechture, eco-consciousness, and social equality. Even during long dark winters, many people find joy in the simple comfort of a warm light in the middle of the dark. With this logo, I sought to represent classic Danish design, the feeling of innovation, and the spirit of hygge.
